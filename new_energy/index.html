Nuclear Energy

Powerful, but volataile.

Highest Possible Energy Output

Nuclear reactors by far have the highest energy output out of anything discussed on this site. Nuclear power provides roughly a fifth of America's electricity (solar, for reference, yields about 1% of the US's power), and that number may go up if fusion becomes practical.

Most Common

Solar panels are able to be set up pretty much anywhere where there's usually light, and the technology has been around for a while, so they're definitely more widespread (in terms of space and volume constructed) than anything else here, even if they don't produce the most energy.

Most Stable

Overall, geothermal energy is the most stable one here because you're drawing heat from the earth itself, as long as you stay there, the nergy won't go anywhere until you run out of it, and that will be a long time in this case. As a result, geothermal energy has a very predictable power output.Solar panels and wind turbines, on the other hand, can be undermined by the weather.
