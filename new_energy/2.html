  • Wind Power is exactly what it sound like. Instead of burning fuels, you simply set up a turbine that can be turned by the wind to generate energy.
In order to get as much wind energy as feasible, New York State has begun commisioning the construction of wind turbines out at sea, investing millions of dollars in these projects.
Texas actually generates more than double the wind energy of any other state. Iowa and Oklahoma also make a lot of it, but they're still nowhere near Texas in that regard.
How big a turbine is can actually be pretty important; the turbines generate more energy with faster winds, so reaching windier altitudes is more important than you'd think.
Pros

Wind power is really cheap, at around 1.5-4 cents per Kilowatt hour, even compared to solar, which just recently reached 6 cents per kilowatt hour. It's efficient enough that it's already responsible for a bit over 9% of US energy production as of 2021.
