  • Geothermal energy relies on heat from deep in the earth to send up steam to- you guessed it!- turn a turbine.
In some cases, Geothermal can actually just be used to heat homes more directly, rather than storing it as energy.
EGS, or enhanced Geothermal Systems, are a new technolgy that uses puped fluid to fracture hot rocks, then takes the heat from the fluid as the rock heats it up.
Geothermal plants can be rather compact, generally occupying less land in proportion to the power they produce than wind farms.
Pros

Geothermal energy is, as you might already know, very predictable and reliable; there aren't really any factors that would cause a fluctuation like with wind and solar.

Cons

Geothermal is really expensive, and 1MW plants regularly cost upwards of two million dollars, sometimes as much as seven millionn dollars.
