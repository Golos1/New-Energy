  • Nuclear power currently relies on fission reactors, needing certain kinds of fuel like uranium, plutonium, and thorium. Nuclear fusion could provide even greater power with more readily available fuel, but it currently takes more enrgy to do safely than it generates.
Nuclear Reactors don't have to be giant buildings; Small Modular Reactors can actually be transported to factories to provide energy for them.
There are only 92 Nuclear reactors in the US, but they're still responsible for roughly 19% of US energy.
Illinois has 11 nuclear reactors, more than any other state, and more than 50% of it's power requirements are met through nuclear energy.
Pros

Nuclear reactors can generate a lot of energy, even compared to fossil fuels ore geothermal energy.

Cons

Current nuclear reactors also create radioactive waste as a byproduct, and in the event of a meltdown, many lives could be lost.
